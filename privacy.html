This Privacy Policy explains how Studlin Labs, Inc. ("Studlin," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects information when you use the Studlin website and application (the "Service"). It is written to align with common data-protection principles, including those reflected in the GDPR and the CCPA/CPRA, but your specific rights depend on where you live — see Section 5.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide directly

  • Account information: name, email address, and (if you sign in with Google) basic profile information such as your profile photo.
  • Academic information: your school or academic institution affiliation, grade/status, and subjects you add.
  • Content you create: tasks, calendar events, routines, notes, flashcards, AI tutor conversations, and any files or notes you choose to share with other users through the Studlin Network.
  • Payment information: if you subscribe to a paid plan, billing details are collected and processed directly by our payment processor, Stripe — Studlin does not receive or store your full card number.
  • Communications: information you provide when you contact us for support.

Information collected automatically

  • Device identifiers, IP address, browser type and version, operating system, and general platform/usage log data necessary to operate, secure, and troubleshoot the Service.
  • A push-notification device token, if you enable notifications, used solely to deliver reminders you've requested.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information described above to:

  • create, maintain, and secure your account and authenticate your sign-in;
  • generate your AI-scheduled study calendar, task prioritization, and related planning features;
  • power the AI tutor and related study-assistance features;
  • calculate school-localized Global Leaderboard rankings and related gamification metrics, using your school affiliation and activity data;
  • enable peer-to-peer note-sharing and study-group collaboration with the specific users/networks you choose to share with;
  • process payments and manage subscriptions;
  • send you service-related communications, including optional push notifications and reminders you request; and
  • maintain the security, integrity, and reliability of the Service, and diagnose technical issues.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your academic content to train third-party AI models beyond what is necessary to generate the response you requested in that session.

4. Data Retention & Security

Your data is stored in Firebase Firestore, using industry-standard TLS/SSL encryption in transit and encryption at rest as provided by our infrastructure providers. We retain your personal information for as long as your account remains active, or as needed to provide the Service, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. If you delete your account, we will delete or anonymize your personal data within a commercially reasonable period, except where retention is required by law.

5. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, export, or request deletion of your personal information, or to object to or restrict certain processing (rights broadly consistent with the GDPR for users in the EU/EEA/UK, and the CCPA/CPRA for California residents).

To exercise any of these rights, send a request to privacy@studlin.app from the email address associated with your account. We will verify your request and respond within the timeframe required by applicable law. You may also delete most of your own content directly within the Service, and permanently delete your account from Settings.

6. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Studlin uses functional, first-party session identifiers set by Firebase Authentication to keep you signed in across visits. These are strictly necessary for the Service to function and are not used for third-party advertising or cross-site tracking. We do not currently use third-party advertising cookies.

7. Children's Privacy

The Service is intended for students generally aged 13 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us personal information without such consent, contact us at privacy@studlin.app so we can address it.

8. International Data Transfers

Our service providers may process and store information in the United States and other countries. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards (such as standard contractual clauses) for cross-border transfers of personal data.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will notify you through the Service or by other reasonable means before the change takes effect.

10. Contact Us

Questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices can be directed to privacy@studlin.app.
