# Studlin AI Development Guide

## What is Studlin?

Studlin is an iOS-first startup built for students.

The mission is simple:

> Make studying significantly less annoying.

Most students use 5-10 different apps:

* Notes
* Flashcards
* Homework trackers
* AI tools
* Calendar
* Timers
* PDF readers
* Group chats

Studlin aims to combine everything students actually need into one ecosystem.

---

# Core Philosophy

## We are NOT another AI wrapper.

AI is a feature.
The student experience is the product.

Every feature should answer:

**"Does this remove friction from a student's life?"**

If not, it probably should not exist.

---

# Brand Personality

Studlin should feel:

* Modern
* Minimal
* Smart
* Student-first
* Motivating
* Clean

Avoid:

* Corporate language
* Academic jargon
* Overly formal copy
* Cringe Gen-Z slang
* Fake productivity guru energy

Good:

> Study smarter.

Better:

> Everything you need to study. One app.

Bad:

> Revolutionize your educational journey through cutting-edge synergistic AI.

---

# Design Principles

## Apple First

The product should feel native to iOS.

Prioritize:

* Large spacing
* Rounded corners
* Smooth animations
* SF Pro typography
* Minimal interfaces
* One primary action per screen

Think:

* Apple
* Linear
* Notion Mobile
* Arc Browser
* Headspace

Avoid clutter.

---

# UX Philosophy

Every screen should answer:

1. Why am I here?
2. What should I do?
3. What happens next?

Never make users think.

---

# Onboarding Philosophy

Goal:

Make the user realize they actually have a study problem.

Flow:

## Introduction

Frame the pain.

Examples:

* Too many apps
* Losing notes
* Missing assignments
* AI tabs everywhere
* No organization

## Climax

Show Studlin solving everything.

Create an "aha moment".

## Conclusion

Small commitment.

Get them into the product quickly.

---

# Copywriting Style

Keep sentences short.

Example:

Bad:
"Leverage artificial intelligence to maximize educational efficiency."

Good:
"Upload your notes.
Get instant study help."

Avoid walls of text.

# Technical Philosophy

Priorities:

1. Simplicity
2. Reliability
3. Speed
4. Maintainability

Avoid overengineering.

If two solutions exist:

Choose the simpler one.

---

# AI Integration Rules

AI should:

* Save time
* Reduce effort
* Feel instant

AI should NOT:

* Replace critical thinking
* Create unnecessary complexity
* Require complex prompting

Users should get value with minimal input.

---

# Feature Evaluation Framework

Before adding a feature, ask:

Does this:

* Save time?
* Reduce stress?
* Remove an app?
* Improve organization?
* Increase focus?

If most answers are no,
do not build it.

---

# UI Rules

Use:

* Large cards
* Generous whitespace
* Soft shadows
* Smooth transitions
* Consistent spacing

Avoid:

* Tiny buttons
* Dense layouts
* Excessive colors
* Dashboard overload

---

# Color Direction

Previous branding used dark themes.

General aesthetic:

* Black
* White
* Neutral grays

Accent colors should feel modern and premium.

Avoid rainbow productivity app aesthetics.

---

# Animation Philosophy

Animations should communicate state.

Examples:

* Cards expanding
* Smooth navigation
* Loading skeletons
* Interactive feedback

Do not animate for the sake of animation.

---

# Development Rules

When generating code:

* Write clean code.
* Prefer readability.
* Use reusable components.
* Keep functions small.
* Avoid duplicate logic.
* Follow existing architecture.
* Preserve design consistency.

# North Star

Studlin should become the app a student opens first and closes last every day.

The goal is not to build more features.

The goal is to remove friction from learning.

When uncertain, ask:

"What would make studying easier for the student right now?"

Build that.
